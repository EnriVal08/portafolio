Alan Turing

Father of Modern Computing

Timeline

  • 1912 – Born on June 23 in Maida Vale, London.
  • 1931–1934 – Studies at the University of Cambridge, excelling in mathematics and logic.
  • 1936 – Publishes "On Computable Numbers," introducing the concept of the Turing machine.
  • 1938 – Earns his Ph.D. at Princeton University.
  • 1939–1945 – Works at Bletchley Park to break the German Enigma code.
  • 1945–1950 – Develops early computers and proposes the "Turing Test" for AI.
  • 1952 – Convicted due to his homosexuality, then illegal in the UK.
  • 1954 – Dies on June 7 under controversial circumstances.

Famous Quotes

“We can only see a short distance ahead, but we can see plenty there that needs to be done.”
“Sometimes it is the people no one can imagine anything of who do the things no one can imagine.”
